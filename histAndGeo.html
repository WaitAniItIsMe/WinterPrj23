History & Geography

Murshidabad, situated in West Bengal, India, has a compelling history and geography which has helped shape its identity throughout the ages. Serving as the final capital of independent Bengal before British colonial rule, the city was founded in the 17th century by Murshid Quli Khan, the viceroy appointed by the Mughal Emperor Akbar.It emerged as a significant hub for trade, administration, and culture, thanks to its strategic location along the Bhagirathi River.
The Battle of Plassey in 1757, a pivotal moment in Indian history, unfolded in this region, leading to British dominance. Murshidabad's architectural heritage, including landmarks like Hazarduari Palace and Katra Masjid, reflects the influences of both Mughal and colonial periods.
